# Swift-Hohenberg Equation

## Equation

The benchmark uses the one-dimensional periodic Swift-Hohenberg equation

$$
u_t=r u-(1+\partial_x^2)^2u-u^3,
$$

on a periodic domain of length $32\pi$, with $r=0.3$.

## Spatial discretization

The implementation uses $N=128$ equispaced points and a Fourier pseudospectral discretization. If $k$ denotes the Fourier wavenumber, the linear part is diagonal in spectral space:

$$
\widehat{L u}(k)=\left[r-(1-k^2)^2\right]\widehat{u}(k).
$$

The cubic nonlinearity is evaluated in physical space and transformed with the FFT. Spatial discretization therefore produces a 128-dimensional stiff ODE system for the time integrators.

## Initial condition

A deterministic multimode perturbation is used:

$$
u(x,0)=0.1\cos(x)+0.01\cos(2x)+0.001\cos(7x).
$$

The default integration interval is $0\le t\le10$.

## Why this benchmark is included

This case complements the repository's low-dimensional ODE tests by exercising:

- scalability to a larger state vector,
- stiffness generated by a fourth-order spatial operator,
- nonlinear pattern-forming dynamics,
- FFT-based Fourier spectral spatial discretization,
- the relative efficiency of explicit and stiff integrators.

## Reference solution

No closed-form solution is used. The framework generates a high-accuracy reference trajectory with its stiff reference solver because the benchmark sets `stiff = true`.

## Recommended metrics

- time-averaged and time-weighted RMS trajectory error,
- maximum trajectory error,
- median repeated elapsed time and timing variability,
- right-hand-side evaluations,
- Jacobian, Newton, and linear-solve counts for implicit methods,
- qualitative agreement of the evolving spatial pattern.
